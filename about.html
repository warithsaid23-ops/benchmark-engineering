Our Story

Built on Experience
Earned Abroad

Benchmark Engineering Co. Ltd was founded in Zanzibar in 2011 with a single conviction: that the islands deserved a construction company held to an international standard. Not a local approximation of it — the genuine article.

Our founding directors returned from the United Kingdom and Russia not with a wish to replicate East African norms, but to disrupt them. They had managed concrete pours on 46-storey London towers, co-ordinated infrastructure works across UK motorway schemes, and delivered projects under some of Europe's most demanding principal contractors. They brought every lesson home.

Fifteen years on, Benchmark has delivered more than fifty projects across Zanzibar, Pemba Island, and the Tanzania mainland — government hospitals, luxury residential towers, commercial fit-outs, and critical island infrastructure — all managed to the same uncompromising standard we set on day one.

We remain independently owned, Zanzibar-registered, and deeply committed to creating skilled employment for Zanzibari people through our Benchmark Pathway programme.

Fifteen Years of Delivery

Our Journey

2011

Company Founded

Benchmark Engineering Co. Ltd incorporated in Zanzibar. Registered with ZCR and TRA. First contracts awarded in building and civil works on Unguja Island.

2013

Government Sector Entry

Awarded first public-sector contracts under ZPPDA procurement. Established Benchmark's reputation for compliant, on-programme government delivery.

2015

Pemba Island Operations

Expanded operations to Pemba Island, becoming one of very few contractors capable of delivering complex projects across both major Zanzibar islands simultaneously.

2017

Healthcare Milestone

Completed Micheweni District Hospital — a TZS 4.8 billion project serving 123,379 people on Pemba Island. The most technically demanding project in the company's history to that date.

2019

Plant Fleet Expansion

Invested in a Class 1-grade plant fleet including Komatsu loaders, tracked excavators, and a full complement of concrete mixers, compactors, and surveying equipment — eliminating subcontractor reliance.

2021

Digital Oversight Introduced

Launched the Benchmark Site Diary — a daily digital reporting system giving clients and directors live visibility of every site across the portfolio. The first system of its kind in East African contracting.

2023

Al-Saadia Apartments Completed

Delivered Al-Saadia multi-storey residential development in Zanzibar City — a landmark scheme demonstrating Benchmark's capability in premium urban residential construction.

2024–25

Pipeline of Landmark Projects

Chukwani Villa luxury residential ongoing. Multiple government contracts under procurement. The Benchmark Pathway training programme formally launched to create a pipeline of Zanzibari construction professionals.

2026

Looking Forward

Fifteen years in, Benchmark continues to set the standard for construction delivery in East Africa — with a growing team, an expanding plant fleet, and an unwavering commitment to the islands we call home.

Leadership

The Directors

Benchmark was built on international experience earned on some of the world's most challenging construction projects — then brought home to Zanzibar.

MA

Mahir Said Ali

Managing Director & Principal Engineer

Mahir trained and built his career in the United Kingdom, working across some of the most technically demanding construction projects in Europe. His career spans engagements with Skanska, BAM Nuttall, Barhale, PC Harriton, and Dean & Dyball — principal contractors recognised internationally for the rigour of their delivery systems.

His UK portfolio includes the Heron Tower, a 46-storey commercial skyscraper in the City of London; major civils works along the River Thames; and the Limehouse Regeneration Programme — a complex urban infrastructure scheme delivering roads, utilities, and public realm across one of London's most congested boroughs.

Returning to Zanzibar in 2011, Mahir founded Benchmark with the explicit goal of transplanting European construction discipline to East Africa — not as an aspiration, but as a non-negotiable operating standard.

Sheikhan S Mohammed

What We Stand For

Mission, Vision & Values

◆

Mission

To deliver every project — regardless of scale — to the highest standard of quality, safety, and programme control available in East Africa. We exist to prove that world-class construction is not geography-dependent.

◆

Vision

To be the contractor of first choice for every significant construction project in Zanzibar and Pemba — trusted by government, preferred by private clients, and recognised across East Africa as the benchmark for project delivery.

◆

Integrity

We report what is happening on site — not what a client wishes to hear. Our daily digital diaries are a commitment to transparency. Difficult conversations are had early, when there is still time to act.

◆

Local Ownership

We are Zanzibari-owned and operated. Every TZS of profit invested back into plant, training, and the communities where we build. Our Benchmark Pathway programme is our commitment to creating the next generation of Zanzibari engineers.

◆

Technical Excellence

Our directors trained under Tier 1 contractors in the UK and Russia. Those standards — rigorous method statements, formal RAMS documentation, daily safety briefings — are applied without compromise to every Benchmark site.

◆

Community First

We hire locally, train deliberately, and measure our contribution not just in completed structures but in the livelihoods of the people who built them. Construction leaves behind more than buildings — it leaves behind skills.
