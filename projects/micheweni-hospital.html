The Challenge

Healthcare Construction at
Island Scale

Micheweni District Hospital is not a routine construction project. It is the primary healthcare facility for an entire district of Pemba Island — an island with limited contractor capacity, constrained supply chains, and no tolerance for programme failures that would delay access to clinical services for 123,379 people.

Healthcare construction demands a specification discipline that most contractors in the region are not equipped to meet. Infection control provisions, patient flow planning, clinical services coordination, and the documentation required by the Ministry of Health and ZPPDA are requirements that cannot be improvised or learned on the job.

The project also required Benchmark to manage a complex cross-island logistics operation — moving materials, plant, and personnel between Zanzibar's main island and Pemba while maintaining programme momentum and cost control on a public-sector contract where financial accountability is non-negotiable.

The Benchmark Approach

Clinical Specification.
Government Compliance.
Island Logistics.

Benchmark's approach to Micheweni was structured around three priorities: clinical specification compliance, government procurement transparency, and island logistics management. These three constraints shaped every decision from the initial programme through to practical completion.

Clinical specification was managed through close coordination with Ministry of Health project supervisors. Every clinical area — wards, theatres, consultation rooms, pharmacy, laboratory — was built to the specification drawings and inspected against them before any subsequent finishes were applied. Change requests from the client during construction were managed through a formal change control process that maintained cost transparency throughout.

Pemba Island operations required Benchmark to establish a semi-permanent site management presence on the island, with materials pre-ordered and staged to avoid supply disruption. Our owned lorry fleet and logistics capability proved essential in maintaining the supply chain across the inter-island crossing.
